Thesis Market Experience Product Measurement

Internal strategy

Connection is the product.

If players feel real human connection, it was a good event. If they do not, it was not valuable. The more connection people feel, the more valuable the experience becomes.

Value logic

Connection turns play into loyalty, referrals, and pricing power.

Silliness is not the end goal. Silliness is the social lubricant that lets lower-level players relax, connect, and decide that this club is where they belong.

01
Players feel safe.

They are not judged for score confusion, weird form, easy misses, or beginner uncertainty.

02
Players connect.

They have a real moment with staff, pros, partners, and other players at their level.

03
Players come back.

The event becomes emotionally meaningful, not just another class on a calendar.

04
Players refer friends.

Five-star connection creates warm word of mouth, higher loyalty, and more willingness to pay.

Market wedge

Lower-level players are the community market.

Competitive players care about ratings, brackets, and quality games. Connection-first players care about whether they feel welcome, whether the room is kind, and whether they have a reason to return.

Five-star experience

A good event is not measured by drills completed. It is measured by connection created.

01 Right place

New and lower-level players feel welcomed, not judged.

02 Caring pros

Instruction builds confidence and community at the same time.

03 Right vibe

Friendly players help social-first players relax and belong.

04 Fitted invitations

Thoughtful invites find the right people, level, and event format.

05 Enough ways in

Clinics, socials, beginner play, and special events make the calendar feel abundant.

06 We listen

Feedback shapes the next experience around players' needs.

Brand mechanism

Silly moments lower the guard.

The name, the score, the kitchen, the wiffle ball, the easy miss, and the paddle tap all tell players the same thing: nobody has to posture here.

Product system

We are building repeatable formats that create connection on purpose.

Score-help social

Make confusion normal. Staff helps players keep score so nobody feels exposed.

Baby block class

Use silly low-stakes drills to make newer players feel capable fast.

Pro plays in

Pros create approachable moments: kitchen lobs, easy balls, playful resets, and confidence-building rallies.

Invitation round

Every good event ends with a prompt: who should be invited next, and by whom?

Academy map

The academy is the packaging for repeatable belonging.

Classes are not just instruction. Each format is a social container that helps lower-level players feel progress, safety, and connection.

Operating KPI

If connection is the value, measure connection.

01 Did they feel welcome?

The first five-star signal for social-first players.

02 Did they meet someone?

The event created real human value, not just court usage.

03 Would they come back?

Loyalty is the practical proof that the experience mattered.

04 Would they bring a friend?

Referral intent is the sales engine hiding inside the community experience.

05 Who should be invited next?

The invitation graph gets smarter after every event.

06 What should staff change?

Feedback turns each event into a better next event.

Internal strategy close

Win the silliness market by owning the five-star connection experience.

The brand promise is simple: let your guard down, meet the right people, and want to come back.
