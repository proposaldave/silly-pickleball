Event products

Clubs get formats, drills, and invitation prompts, not just a logo.

7112
Score-Help Social

For players uncomfortable keeping score. Staff leads approachable games and makes score confusion normal.

  • Players can laugh when nobody knows if it is 8-6-2 or 6-8-1.
  • Asking for score help becomes a community moment, not embarrassment.
Ball Striking That Works With My Technique and Ability

Players learn a swing and contact plan that fits their body, level, timing, and confidence instead of copying one generic stroke.

  • The silly part is that weird technique can work if it fits the player.
  • Players stop pretending everyone needs the same perfect-looking swing.
Baby Block Class

Players try to get the ball past the pro from kitchen line to kitchen line, then use five-hole targets to learn that tiny calm blocks can beat big swings.

  • Students blast balls from 14 feet away while Mike Crosby wears safety eyewear.
  • Five-hole targets live inside this class, not as a separate product.
Cover the Middle + Tic Tac Toe

Teams use a tic-tac-toe target map to learn who owns middle balls, when to call mine, and how to cover space together.

  • The silly part is that two adults can both guard the same square while the ball lands between them.
  • Players win the drill by talking early, covering the middle, and making the center square feel shared.
Mastering the Resets

Players learn to survive pace, soften the ball, and move forward from the transition zone instead of panicking.

  • Players at the kitchen line get to hit overheads at the pro.
  • The pro has to defend from the transition zone and turn chaos into resets.
Patience

Players win points by staying soft until someone gets impatient and rolls the dice too soon.

  • Adults softly tap a plastic ball like it is precious.
  • The class turns boredom, panic, and patience into a game.
Let It Go Out-Ball Class

Greg Bennett uses the ball machine to feed tempting out-balls so players learn judgment, restraint, and the confidence to let bad balls fly.

  • The funny part is that players win the rep by doing absolutely nothing.
  • Everyone practices resisting the urge to rescue a ball that was already going out.
Trick Shots Lab

A playful clinic for net-cord touch, Caleb's blow shot, Ernes, and around-the-post shots.

  • Players try the Caleb-style net-cord blow shot when the ball hangs near the tape.
  • Ernes and around-the-post shots become silly experiments, not advanced-player posturing.
Pro Plays In

The pro joins member games and floats kitchen lobs so players get silly chances to finish easy balls.

  • The pro creates a sitter and everyone watches if the player can finish it.
  • Missing an easy ball becomes funny instead of shameful.
Kitchen Sitter Lab

Players practice the ball everyone thinks is automatic, then reset when the sitter still finds a way to get missed.

  • The whole point is practicing the easy ball people somehow miss.
  • Everyone learns that "automatic" is usually not automatic.

Middle Mystery

Teams earn points for clear calls, coverage, and recovering from "who's got middle?" confusion.

  • "I thought you had it" becomes a drill instead of a relationship test.
  • Two players staring at the same ball becomes shared language.
Weird Works

Players score bonus points for awkward saves, mishits, tweeners, resets, and ugly balls that stay alive.

  • Frame hits, handle saves, and ugly blocks count if they stay low.
  • The class celebrates points that look wrong but work.
Invitation Round

After the event, players get a simple prompt to invite the right friend back into the next silly format.

  • The last rally becomes a question: who should come next?
  • The silly moment turns into a warm referral.
