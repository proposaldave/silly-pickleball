Silly Pickleball Academy curriculum

Baby Block Class

A repeatable class package for helping newer and intermediate players feel safe, skilled, and fun to play with. The goal is not just better technique. The goal is more Give n Receive: calmer partners, longer rallies, softer hands, and silly moments that lower the guard.

Class promise

Players learn that tiny calm blocks can beat big swings.

Who it is for

Lower-level and developing players who panic when pace comes at them, pop balls up, or feel embarrassed at the kitchen line.

Five-star outcome

Players leave feeling safer, more useful to partners, and more willing to show up again because the class made defense social and funny.

Silly hook

The pro baby blocks, lets out-balls go, and makes the drill feel like a game instead of a technical lecture.

Class flow

Run the same emotional arc every time.

  1. Welcome and score-safe framing. Make it clear nobody is here to be judged. The room is for calm hands, funny misses, and getting one ball better.
  2. Demo the tiny motion. Pro shows big swing versus baby block. Players see the ball die softly instead of flying.
  3. Kitchen-line baby block. Players try to get the ball by the pro from kitchen line to kitchen line while the pro baby blocks and lets out-balls go.
  4. Five-hole challenge. Players aim through the middle gap. The silly target makes touch memorable and lowers pressure.
  5. Feet target transfer. Players block to the opponent's feet so defense becomes a way to give partners a better next ball.
  6. Live-play invite. End by pairing people who should play again and naming the next class, social, or practice path.

Curriculum modules

Each class module needs skill, silly moment, and connection purpose.

Baby block basics

Ready position, quiet paddle, short motion, firm enough grip, and no panic swing.

Let out-balls go

Teach players that value comes from judgment and calm, not touching every ball.

Block to feet

Turn defense into a generous ball that helps the team, not just survival.

Fill and follow-up

The curriculum is also the sales process.

Before class

Invite the anxious player by name: this is a safe class for learning soft hands, not a competitive test.

During class

Notice who laughed, who relaxed, who helped someone else, and who should be paired again.

After class

Send the next invite: another Baby Block, Dink Forever, Score-Help Social, or Pro Plays In.

Host script

"Today is about becoming more fun to play with. If you can take a hard ball and give your partner a calm next ball, people will want you on their court. We are going to make that feel simple, silly, and safe."
